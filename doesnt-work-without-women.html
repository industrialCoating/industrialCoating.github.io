This Doesn’t Work Without Them

By Anees Merzi | March 2026

Everyone sees the kids.

The goals. The drills. The games on the weekend.

Some people see the coaches too.

But almost no one sees what actually holds all of it together.

Because the truth is… this doesn’t work without them.

The Part People Get Wrong

You can walk around most youth sports environments and see how it’s framed.

Coaches are mostly men. Leadership is mostly men. And on the surface, it looks like dads are the ones driving the experience.

That’s the story people tell themselves.

But that’s not the full story.

Because behind the scenes, quietly and consistently, there’s another layer doing the real work.

Not for credit. Not for recognition. Just because it needs to get done.

You Can’t Outwork a Missing Ecosystem

I can do a lot on my own.

Coach sessions. Organize teams. Build programs. Show up every single week.

But none of that matters if the ecosystem isn’t there.

Effort alone doesn’t sustain something like this.

People do.

And at 703 Warriors, that system is powered by women.

What That Actually Looks Like

It looks like a mom getting off a long day of work and still making sure her child gets to practice.

It looks like rides being coordinated, schedules being managed, and trust being given when it matters most.

It looks like volunteers stepping in to run events, help organize chaos, and make things feel structured and safe for the kids.

It looks like coaches and mentors who lead with patience, who teach more than just the game, and who understand what these kids actually need.

And it looks like partners, like the women at Dominion Guild, who believe in what we’re building and step in when others don’t.

None of this is loud.

But all of it is essential.

What the Kids Are Really Learning

The kids may not say it out loud, but they see it.

They see who shows up. Who organizes. Who leads. Who cares.

They see strength that doesn’t need to announce itself.

They see consistency.

They see what leadership actually looks like in real life.

And that shapes how they move through the world.

Let’s Be Clear

This isn’t support.

This isn’t “helping out.”

This isn’t something extra on the side.

This is the foundation.

Without it, there are no practices. No structure. No consistency. No growth.

No 703 Warriors.

This Month, We Say It Out Loud

Not as a formality. Not as a post. Not as a box to check.

But because it’s the truth.

This works because of you.

And we don’t take that lightly.

Bigger Than This Program

If we’re serious about access, about community, about changing what youth sports can look like…

Then we have to be honest about who is actually doing the work.

At 703 Warriors, we don’t just celebrate women during Women’s Month.

We build with them. Every single day.
